Mules

When I was a waitress, I had to learn what drinks to recommend to my tables. I found that mules were a huge crowd pleaser. They don't taste too strongly like liquor, they're not overly sweet, and it's easy to customize them to your taste by using different flavors of vodka or different liquors. They're also really easy to make, with just three ingredients! Though they are typically served in copper mugs, they taste just as good in a regular short glass if that's all you have.

Classic Moscow Mule

The original mule: vodka, lime juice, and ginger beer. If you like spicy, add 2-3 slices of jalapeño before you put ice in the glass.

Ingredients:

  • 2 ounces of vodka
  • half-ounce of lime juice (about half a lime, squeezed)
  • about 4 ounces of ginger beer
HINT: if you don't have a jigger (the instrument that bartenders use to measure shots), you can use a tablespoon measure! 1 ounce = 2 tablespoons.

How to make it:

  1. Add ice to the glass, then add the vodka and lime juice.
  2. Fill the glass the rest of the way with ginger beer.
  3. Stir the drink with a spoon.
  4. Enjoy!

Variations

Kentucky Mule:

Substitute bourbon for vodka.

Mexican Mule:

Substitute reposado tequila for vodka.

Dark'n'Stormy:

Omit the ice and substitute a dark spiced rum for vodka.
