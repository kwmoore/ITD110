Sweets & Sours

Just like people, some drinks are sweet, some are sour, but most of them are some combination of both! The trick to making these cocktails is getting the right ratio of sweet to sour, which usually depends on your own taste. If you like sweet, add more sweetener- if you don't, add less.

Classic Margarita

The important thing to remember with the margarita is that you will taste the liquor- so make sure you're using a good tequila (100% agave!). To make the drink go down even more smoothly, use a nice orange liqeuer like Cointreau in place of triple sec.

Image at right courtesy of Andreas Zieroth on Flickr, CC BY-SA 2.0.

Ingredients:

  • 2 ounces of tequila
  • 1 ounce of lime juice (about one lime, squeezed)
  • half-ounce of agave
  • half-ounce of orange liqueur
TIP: if you don't have a cocktail shaker, use a to-go coffee mug!

How to make it:

  1. Fill your shaker with ice, then add all the ingredients.
  2. Shake for 15-30 seconds.
  3. Add fresh ice to a short glass and strain the contents of the shaker into the glass.
  4. Enjoy!
Variations
  • Spicy Margarita: Add 2-3 slices of jalapeño to the shaker before you add ice and muddle them to release the heat.

Classic Mojito

This is one of my all-time favorite drinks. You can add as much or as little simple syrup to the mixture depending on how sweet you want it to be.

Image at right courtesy of California Bakery on Flickr, CC BY-SA 2.0.

Ingredients:

  • 2 ounces of white rum
  • 1 small handful of mint leaves
  • 1 ounce of lime juice
  • 1 ounce of simple syrup
  • Club soda (just a little bit)

      • How to make it:

      1. Put your mint in a pint glass. Add the lime juice and simple syrup. (I like to mix the lime juice and simple syrup in a separate vessel before adding it, to make sure it tastes the way I want it to.)
      2. Muddle the mint leaves by pushing them against the sides and bottom of the glass.
        3. TIP: bartenders use a tool called a muddler- if you don't have one, a spoon is just fine.
      3. Add the rum, then fill the glass with ice. Top off the glass with club soda.
      4. Mix the drink by pouring it into a different glass, then pour it back into the original glass. It's best not to overmix a mojito.
      5. Enjoy!
      Variations
      • Vodka mint limeade: Substitute vodka for rum.
      • Basil mojito: Substitute basil for mint.
      • Spicy mojito: Muddle jalapeños with the mint, lime, and simple syrup mixture.
      • Elderflower mojito: Use just 1 ounce of rum (or vodka) and 1 ounce of elderflower liqueur.

Mint Julep

While we're talking about cocktails with mint...

Ingredients

  • 2 ounces of bourbon
  • 1 small handful of mint leaves
  • half-ounce of simple syrup

How to make it:

  1. Add the mint leaves and simple syrup to a short glass and muddle.
  2. Add the bourbon and ice, and stir with a spoon until the glass starts getting frosty.
  3. Enjoy!

Whiskey Sour

Warning: this cocktail requires you to have an open mind about drinking an egg white... (Don't worry- if you don't want to include the egg white, you don't have to, but the drink won't be as foamy.

Ingredients

  • 2 ounces of bourbon
  • 1 ounce of lemon juice (that's most of the juice from one lemon)
  • half-ounce of simple syrup
  • 1 egg white

How to separate an egg white:

How to make it:

  1. Fill a shaker with ice and add all the ingredients.
  2. Add fresh ice to a short glass, then strain the shaker into the glass.
  3. Add a couple dashes of Angostura bitters if you'd like, for a more complex flavor.
  4. Enjoy!
Variations
  • Pisco sour: Substitute pisco for bourbon and lime juice for lemon juice.
  • Amaretto sour: Substitute amaretto liqueur for bourbon.
  • Gin fizz: Substitute gin for bourbon.
